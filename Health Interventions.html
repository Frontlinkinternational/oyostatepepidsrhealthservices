Key Focus Areas:

• Reducing risk factors (tobacco, alcohol, unhealthy diet, air pollution)
• Tackling noncommunicable diseases (NCDs) like hypertension, diabetes
• Improving mental health services
• Addressing social determinants of health

PROVIDING HEALTH SERVICES (UNIVERSAL HEALTH COVERAGE – UHC)
Ensuring everyone has access to quality health services without financial hardship

Key Focus Areas:
• Strengthening primary health care (PHC) systems
• Expanding routine immunization programmes
• Improving access to essential medicines and vaccines
• Enhancing health workforce capacity
• Digital health and health system innovation

1. Improve Reproductive, Maternal, Newborn and Child Health (RMNCH) and Nutrition
This priority focuses on reducing preventable deaths and improving health outcomes across the life cycle of women, newborns, and children.

Key focus areas:
• Strengthen antenatal, delivery, and postnatal care services
• Increase access to skilled birth attendants and emergency obstetric care
• Improve newborn care (early breastfeeding, neonatal resuscitation, care for preterm babies)
• Scale up child survival interventions (IMCI, ORS, zinc, vitamin A)
• Address malnutrition through maternal nutrition, infant and young child feeding practices, and treatment of acute malnutrition

Expected outcomes:
• Reduced maternal, neonatal, and under five mortality
• Improved nutritional status of women and children
• Healthier pregnancies and early childhood development

2. Accelerate Immunization Programs for Priority Antigens
This priority aims to protect children and communities from vaccine preventable diseases.

Key focus areas:
• Expand routine immunization coverage for priority vaccines (e.g. DPT3, polio, measles, yellow fever)
• Strengthen outreach to hard to reach and underserved populations
• Improve cold chain and vaccine logistics management
• Address vaccine hesitancy through community engagement and communication

Expected outcomes:
• Increased immunization coverage
• Reduced incidence of vaccine preventable diseases
• Prevention of outbreaks and epidemics

3. Slow Down the Growth Rate of Non Communicable Diseases (NCDs)
This priority addresses the rising burden of chronic diseases.

Key focus areas:
• Promote healthy lifestyles (physical activity, healthy diets, reduced tobacco and alcohol use)
• Early screening for hypertension, diabetes, cancers, and mental health conditions
• Integration of NCD services into primary health care
• Strengthen referral systems and continuity of care

Expected outcomes:
• Early detection and management of NCDs
• Reduced complications and premature deaths
• Lower long term healthcare costs

4. Reduce the Incidence of HIV, Tuberculosis, and Malaria
This priority targets major communicable diseases that contribute significantly to morbidity and mortality.

Key focus areas:
• Expand HIV testing, treatment, and prevention services
• Improve TB case detection, treatment adherence, and drug resistant TB management
• Scale up malaria prevention (bed nets, indoor residual spraying) and effective treatment
• Strengthen community based and key population interventions

Expected outcomes:
• Reduced new infections
• Improved survival and quality of life
• Progress toward disease elimination goals

5. Improve Quality of Care and Service Delivery in Primary Health Care Centers (PHCs)
PHCs are the foundation of the health system.

Key focus areas:
• Upgrade PHC infrastructure and essential equipment
• Ensure availability of essential medicines and supplies
• Improve clinical standards, patient safety, and supervision
• Strengthen community trust and utilization of PHC services

Expected outcomes:
• Increased use of PHC services
• Improved patient satisfaction and health outcomes
• Reduced burden on secondary and tertiary facilities

6. Expand Financial Protection Through Health Insurance and Innovative Financing
This priority reduces financial hardship related to healthcare.

Key focus areas:
• Expand population coverage through health insurance schemes
• Protect vulnerable groups and informal sector workers
• Reduce out of pocket expenditure
• Introduce innovative financing mechanisms (e.g. pooled funds, subsidies)

Expected outcomes:
• Improved access to care regardless of ability to pay
• Reduction in catastrophic health spending
• Stronger and more sustainable health financing system

7. Increase Availability and Quality of Human Resources for Health (HRH)
A strong health system depends on skilled and motivated workers.

Key focus areas:
• Recruitment and deployment of adequate health workers
• Continuous training and capacity development
• Retention strategies for rural and underserved areas
• Performance management and supportive supervision

Expected outcomes:
• Improved service coverage and quality
• Reduced workforce shortages
• Enhanced morale and productivity

8. Stimulate Local Production of Health Products
This priority strengthens health security and self reliance.

Key focus areas:
• Support local manufacturing of essential medicines and vaccines
• Encourage production of medical supplies (e.g. bed nets, diagnostics, therapeutics)
• Improve regulatory and quality assurance systems
• Foster public–private partnerships and innovation

Expected outcomes:
• Reduced dependence on imports
• Improved supply security and affordability
• Job creation and economic growth

9. Revitalize Tertiary and Quaternary Hospitals for Specialized Care
This priority ensures access to advanced and specialized medical services.

Key focus areas:
• Upgrade diagnostic and treatment technologies
• Strengthen specialized clinical training and research
• Improve referral and counter referral systems
• Enhance governance and efficiency of tertiary facilities

Expected outcomes:
• Better management of complex conditions
• Reduced need for medical tourism
• Stronger centers of excellence

10. Improve Pandemic Prevention, Detection, Preparedness and Response
This priority builds resilience to public health emergencies.

Key focus areas:
• Strengthen disease surveillance and early warning systems
• Build laboratory and diagnostic capacity
• Develop emergency preparedness and response plans
• Improve risk communication and community engagement

Expected outcomes:
• Faster detection of outbreaks
• More effective emergency response
• Reduced health, social, and economic impact of pandemics

11. Improve Regulation and Regulatory Processes
Strong regulation ensures safety, quality, and trust in the health system.

Key focus areas:
• Strengthen regulation of medicines, vaccines, and medical devices
• Improve licensing and accreditation of health facilities and professionals
• Enhance enforcement and transparency
• Streamline regulatory processes to reduce delays

Expected outcomes:
• Higher safety and quality standards
• Increased confidence in health services and products
• Reduced circulation of substandard and counterfeit products

12. Strengthen Health Data Collection, Reporting and Use
Data driven decision making is essential for effective health systems.

Key focus areas:
• Improve routine health information systems
• Standardize core indicators and reporting tools
• Build capacity for data analysis and use at all levels
• Use data for planning, monitoring, and accountability

Expected outcomes:
• Better evidence based decisions
• Improved monitoring of health programs
• Enhanced transparency and accountability

13. Accelerate Inter Sectoral Social Welfare Through the Social Action Fund
Health outcomes are influenced by social determinants.

Key focus areas:
• Coordinate health with social welfare, education, and other sectors
• Target vulnerable populations with integrated support
• Improve social protection and emergency assistance mechanisms
• Strengthen governance and coordination of the Social Action Fund

Expected outcomes:
• Reduced health inequities
• Improved social inclusion and resilience
• Stronger multisectoral collaboration
