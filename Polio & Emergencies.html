  • OYO STATE POLIO & HEALTH EMERGENCIES EFFORTS:
  • KEY MESSAGE
  • Polio eradication is a global public health success story, with Africa now free of wild poliovirus.
  • Although cVPV is still a challenge in Nigeria, the last case for Oyo State was confirmed in the year 2025. With strong measures recently ongoing to ensure sustained population immunity so outbreak of cVPV2 continnnue to be a history in Oyo state
  • The same systems used for polio are now critical for detecting and responding to other infectious diseases.
  • Sustaining high immunization coverage is essential to prevent both polio and other vaccine-preventable diseases.
  • OYO State of Nigeria and Nigeria
  • Polio eradication efforts in Oyo State and Nigeria are among the most extensive in the world and have achieved major milestones, including interruption of wild poliovirus transmission
  • Key Activities:
  • Mass Immunization Campaigns (SIAs):
  • Nationwide house-to-house vaccination targeting children under 5 years.
  • Routine Immunization Strengthening:
  • Integration of Oral Polio Vaccine (OPV) into national immunization schedules.
  •  Emergency Operations Centres (EOCs):
  • Coordinating outbreak response and monitoring progress at national and state levels.
  • Acute Flaccid Paralysis (AFP) Surveillance:
  • Detecting suspected polio cases through health facilities and community informants.
  • Environmental Surveillance:
  • Testing sewage samples for poliovirus circulation.
  • Community Engagement & Social Mobilization:
  • Involving religious leaders, traditional rulers, and volunteers.
  • Cross-border Surveillance & Vaccination:
  • Targeting mobile and hard-to-reach populations.
  • Rapid Surveillance Assessments (RSA):
  • Strengthening detection systems and closing surveillance gaps.
  • Polio Emergency Action Plan (NPEAP):
  • Strategic framework guiding eradication activities.
  • Africa (African Region): We align with the regional office activities as highlighted below:
  • Key Activities:
  • • “Kick Polio Out of Africa” Campaign (1996):
  • Political and community mobilization across the continent.
  • • Mass Vaccination of Millions of Children:
  • Reaching even remote and conflict-affected areas.
  • • Integrated Surveillance Systems:
  • Laboratory networks and field surveillance teams across countries.
  • • Outreach to Hard-to-Reach Populations:
  • Mobile teams, transit vaccination points, and community informants.
  • • Use of Polio Infrastructure for Other Diseases:
  • Supporting responses to Ebola, COVID-19, measles, cholera, etc.
  • • Containment of Vaccine-Derived Poliovirus (cVDPV): Targeted immunization in low-coverage areas.
  • Global (Global Polio Eradication Initiative – GPEI)
  • We also align with the Global Polio eradication Initiative (GPEI)
  • Launched in 1988, the global initiative has reduced polio cases by over 99% worldwide.
  • Key Activities:
  • • Global Immunization Strategy:
  • Ensuring every child receives multiple doses of polio vaccine.
  • • Polio Endgame Strategy (2019–2023 & beyond):
  • o Eradication (vaccination + surveillance)
  • o Integration (health system strengthening)
  • o Containment (preventing re-emergence)
  • • Global Laboratory Network:
  • Rapid detection and confirmation of poliovirus.
  • • Outbreak Response Campaigns:
  • Rapid vaccination following detection of cases.
  • • Partnership Coordination:
  • WHO, UNICEF, CDC, Rotary International, Gates Foundation.
  • Monitoring & Certification:
  • Regions declared polio-free after ≥3 years without cases.
  • EPIDEMIC-PRONE DISEASES (IDSR PRIORITY DISEASES)
  • Under the Integrated Disease Surveillance and Response (IDSR) framework, the following epidemic-prone diseases are prioritized:
  • Viral Diseases:
  • • Ebola Virus Disease (EVD)
  • • Lassa Fever
  • • Yellow Fever
  • • Measles
  • • COVID-19
  • • Dengue Fever
  • Bacterial Diseases:
  • • Cholera
  • • Cerebrospinal Meningitis
  • • Plague
  • • Typhoid Fever
  • Other Priority Conditions:
  • • Acute Viral Hemorrhagic Fevers
  • • Influenza (Pandemic-prone)
  • These diseases require immediate reporting, rapid response, and outbreak investigation.
  • 💉 VACCINE-PREVENTABLE DISEASES (VPDs)
  • These are diseases targeted through routine immunization and supplementary campaigns:
  • Childhood Vaccine-Preventable Diseases:
  • • Poliomyelitis (Polio)
  • • Measles
  • • Tuberculosis (BCG)
  • • Diphtheria
  • • Pertussis (Whooping cough)
  • • Tetanus
  • • Hepatitis B
  • • Haemophilus influenzae type b (Hib)
  • • Pneumococcal disease
  • • Rotavirus diarrhea
  • Additional VPDs (Campaign or Special Programs):
  • • Yellow Fever
  • • Human Papillomavirus (HPV)
  • • Meningococcal meningitis
  • • COVID-19
  • 🔗 LINK BETWEEN POLIO PROGRAMME & IDSR
  • Polio eradication infrastructure supports broader disease surveillance:
  • • Strengthened surveillance systems used for multiple diseases
  • • Shared laboratory networks
  • • Rapid response teams for outbreaks
  • • Community-based reporting systems
  • Polio programmes have significantly contributed to controlling diseases like measles, cholera, Ebola, and COVID-19
