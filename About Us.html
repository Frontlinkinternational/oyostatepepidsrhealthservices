The Oyo State PEP, IDSR and Health Services are conducted in line with the WHO strategy, FMOH, NCDC and NPHCDA, we work in collaboration with different Government MDAs, and partners to promote health, keep the world safe and serve the vulnerable . We prevent, detect, respond and control infectious disease outbreaks and public health emergencies.
1. Emergency Preparedness and Response: Assists in managing health emergencies, especially in conflict-affected areas (like the northeast). Coordinates health support during floods, disease outbreaks, and humanitarian crises.
2. Disease Prevention and Control Supports vaccination campaigns (e.g., polio, measles, Vaccines Preventable Diseases, COVID-19). Helps Oyo State to respond to disease outbreaks (e.g., Vaccines Preventable Diseases like: cholera, Lassa fever, yellow fever). Works on providing technical supports to malaria control, HIV/AIDS, and tuberculosis programs.
3. Immunization and Polio Eradication WHO in Collaboration with Oyo State Government Coordinates the Global Polio Eradication Initiative in Oyo State
4. Health System Strengthening: Provide technical support to improve primary healthcare delivery. Works with Oyo’s Ministry of Health to develop policies, strategies, and training.
Supports data collection and use of health information systems.
5. Maternal and Child Health Promotes safe childbirth, newborn care, and access to essential services for mothers and children. Supports nutrition and growth monitoring.
Helped Oyo and Nigeria eliminate wild poliovirus in 2020.
6. Health Promotion and Education Encourages healthy behaviors through public education, especially on hygiene, nutrition, and disease prevention.
7. Partnership and Coordination Collaborates with the State Ministry of Health, local authorities, NGOs, and other UN agencies. Mobilizes resources and coordinates donor support for health programs.
